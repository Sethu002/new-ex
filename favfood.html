RECIPE DETAILS

  • The earliest written record of noodles is found in a book dated to the Eastern Han period (25–220 CE).
  • Noodles made from wheat dough became a prominent food for the people of the Han dynasty.
  • The oldest evidence of noodles was from 4,000 years ago in China.
  • Ramen noodles, based on southern Chinese noodle dishes from Guangzhou but named after the northern Chinese lamian, became common in Japan by 1900.

INGRADIENTS

  1. 2 tbsp oil
  2. 2 clove garlic (finely chopped)
  3. 1 inch ginger (finely chopped)
  4. 1 chilli (slit)
  5. ½ onion (sliced)
  6. 2 tbsp spring onion (chopped)
  7. ½ carrot (chopped)
  8. ¼ green capsicum (chopped)
  9. 5 snow pea (chopped)
  10. 5 beans (chopped)
  11. ¼ red capsicum (chopped)
  12. 5 florets broccoli
  13. 3 tbsp cabbage (shredded)
  14. 2 tbsp tomato sauce
  15. 1 tbsp soy sauce
  16. 1 tbsp vinegar
  17. 1 tbsp chilli sauce
  18. ¼ tsp pepper powder
  19. ½ tsp salt

STEPS TO PREPARE

  • Boil water in a large pot. Once the water is boiling, add the noodles and cook according to the package instructions. Stir occasionally to prevent the noodles from sticking together.
  • While the noodles are cooking, you can prepare any vegetables or proteins you would like to add to your dish. Cut them into small pieces so they cook quickly.
  • Once the noodles are done cooking, drain them in a colander and rinse them under cold water to stop the cooking process.
  • Heat up some oil in a pan and add your vegetables or proteins. Cook them until they are tender.
  • Add the cooked noodles to the pan with the vegetables or proteins and stir everything together.
  • Add your desired seasoning, such as soy sauce, salt, pepper, or other flavorings, to taste.
  • Serve hot and enjoy your delicious noodles!
